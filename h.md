**Euler's Formula** establishes a fundamental relationship between trigonometric functions and the complex exponential function:

`e^(ix) = cos(x) + i*sin(x)`

Where:
*   `e` is the base of the natural logarithm
*   `i` is the imaginary unit
*   `cos` and `sin` are the trigonometric functions cosine and sine, respectively
*   `x` is a real number (or complex number in a more general case)

A special and highly celebrated case of Euler's formula is **Euler's Identity**, which occurs when `x = π`:

`e^(iπ) + 1 = 0`

This identity is renowned for connecting five fundamental mathematical constants: `e`, `i`, `π`, `1`, and `0`, using only the basic arithmetic operations of addition, multiplication, and exponentiation. It is often called "the most beautiful formula in mathematics."
